About the Author

My name is Aaron Allen, a current Senior at Del Valle High School. I will be attending the University of Texas next fall and majoring in Computer Science. I have had a hard time getting to where I am at today. I have Asperger Syndrome, severe anxiety and depression. I was bullied a lot in school and eventually got into a very dark mental state. I got out of it through the support of my family, friends, and my Service Dog named Roxi. I went from being in in-school-shutdown everyday to being in class everyday. I have served as an officer in Student Council, Senior Class Council, and the National Honor Society. I came out of the darkness and went to and stayed in the light. I'm here to help you do that, too.

Types of Mental Illnesses

According to the Mayo Clinic, there are many types of mental illnesses, including Anxiety, Clincical Depression, Autism, Bipolar Disorder, ADHD, OCD, Demensia, Schizophrenia, and PTSD. All of these have different symptoms and challenges. One thing that the disabilities listed above have in common are that they originate in the brain either through inherated traits, abnormal change in brain chemistry, or abnormal exposures to items in the environment of birth. Symptoms of mental illnesses include confused thinking or reduced ability to concentrate, excessive fears, worries, feelings of guilt, or mood changes, withdrawal from friends and activities, detachment from reality (delusions), paranoia or hallucinations, inability to cope with daily problems or stress, trouble understanding and relating to situations and to people, and suicidal thoughts.
